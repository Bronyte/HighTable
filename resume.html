Steps To Building a Strong Resume

  • Career

Introduction

Building a strong resume is an ongoing process. Tailoring it to each job application and continually improving it will increase your chances of standing out to potential employers.


Tips for Success

    Understand the Purpose of Your Resume:

  • Before you start, understand that a resume's purpose is to showcase your qualifications, skills, and experiences to potential employers. It should convince them that you are a strong candidate for the job.

    • Choose the Right Resume Format:

  • Select a resume format that suits your background and the industry you're targeting. Common formats include chronological, functional, and combination (hybrid).

    • Gather Your Information:

  • Collect all relevant information, such as personal details, work history, education, skills, certifications, and any other qualifications.

    • Create a Header:

  • Start with a header that includes your full name, phone number, email address, and optionally, a link to your LinkedIn profile or personal website.

    • Write a Compelling Summary or Objective:

  • Craft a concise summary or objective statement that highlights your key qualifications and career goals. Tailor it to the specific job you're applying for.

    • List Your Education:

  • Include your educational background, starting with your most recent degree. Include the institution's name, degree earned, graduation date, and GPA (if it's strong).

    • Detail Your Work Experience:

  • List your work history in reverse chronological order (most recent job first). For each position, provide the job title, company name, location, employment dates, and a bullet-point list of key responsibilities and achievements.

    • Highlight Achievements:

  • Focus on quantifiable achievements and results in your work experience. Use action verbs to describe your accomplishments and demonstrate your impact.

    • Showcase Relevant Skills:

  • Create a skills section that highlights both technical and soft skills relevant to the job. These could include technical skills (e.g., programming languages) and soft skills (e.g., teamwork, communication).

    • Include Additional Sections (Optional):

  • Depending on your background, you might add sections for awards, certifications, publications, projects, or volunteer experience.

    • Tailor Your Resume:

  • Customize your resume for each job application. Modify your summary, skills, and achievements to align with the specific job requirements.

    • Use Action Words:

  • Use strong action words (e.g., "achieved," "managed," "implemented") to start your bullet points. This creates a more impactful impression.

    • Quantify Achievements:

  • Whenever possible, use numbers or percentages to quantify your achievements. This adds credibility to your accomplishments.

    • Check for Consistency:

  • Ensure consistent formatting, such as using the same font and spacing throughout your resume. Consistency creates a polished and professional look.

    • Proofread Carefully:

  • Review your resume for spelling and grammar errors. Typos can make a negative impression on employers.

    • Get Feedback:

  • Share your resume with friends, mentors, or career advisors to get feedback and suggestions for improvement.

    • Formatting and Layout:

  • Use a clean and readable layout. Employ clear headings and bullet points for easy scanning. Avoid overly creative or elaborate designs unless you're in a creative field.

    • Save in the Right Format:

  • Save your resume as a PDF to ensure that it retains its formatting when viewed on different devices and operating systems.

    • Name Your File:

  • Use a clear and professional naming convention for your resume file, such as "FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf."

    • Upload and Send:

  • Use your tailored resume when applying for jobs online. Ensure that you follow the specific instructions provided by each employer regarding the submission of your resume.

    • Update Regularly:

  • Keep your resume up to date with your latest experiences and skills.
